About Rebel Pool

Rebel Pool is built by coders, bootstrapped, and run for stakers first. No outside investors, no fee switches waiting to flip, no marketing burn. Just transparent contracts and honest math.

Why “Pool raises $20m” is a Red Flag

Venture investors don’t buy yield, they demand it. When a protocol raises outside money, future cashflows are needs to hit venture-style returns.
That return has to come from somewhere and there is only from staker yield!.

  • Liquidation preferences (e.g., 1–2×): investors get paid first, stakers later.
  • Governance capture: fee switches, token mints, and treasury taps become “strategic options.”
  • Marketing burn: Eye-candy and Hype don’t compound your principal.
  • Roadmap drift: growth-at-all-costs > sustainable APY.

Signal, not noise: fundraise headlines rarely mean “more yield for stakers.” Treat them as a caution sign.

Code Trust: The Truth Is in the Code

Our contracts are open and simple to verify:
• StakePoolCore holds the underlying (WMON), tracks assets, shares, and the index.
• AquaMON (stMON) is the rebasing facade over shares.
• ArcMON (wstMON) is a non-rebasing wrapper over Aqua for DeFi/governance.

No hidden waterfalls. No backdoors. If something matters, it’s on-chain and reviewable.

Eye-Candy Doesn’t Raise Returns

Animated gradients don’t create APR. They cost time and money. We keep a straightforward UI so resources go where they should—into safe, efficient contracts and better staking mechanics.
